Kiwii Game Portfolio

Genres I enjoy

  • Fighting Games

    • I have always been a fan of fighting games because they have a large replay value due to them having large rosters (which can be expanded upon).

    They work well as both a casual and competitive game as the controls can be mastered through combos or players can simply noodle around on their controllers or button mash.

    Complexity can be found through learning in game frame data, character hitboxes, etc which makes a rewarding system for players to ioncrease their own skill.

    I have always wanted to make my own fighting game because I am fascinated by the mechanics and would like to make my own unique fighting game to add to the genre.


  • Adventure Games

    • I love adventure games...

    The free feeling of exploration and a grand journey have always appealed to me and as such I have always been drawn to games that allow players

    to do their own thing and choose how they go about playing the game.

    Having a sense of freedom, yet still subtly guiding players down the right path is a game design skill that I admire greatly.


  • Stealth Games

    • Stealth Games are one of the more niche genres in the gaming industry yet, I have always wanted to make my own due to my interest

    in the mechanics behind making a game where interaction and fighting is discouraged.

    Making these games fair to the player yet not too easy that it breaks the immersion,

    using different vision cones and pathfinding algorithms to vary enemy difficulty to push the player, it all seems so fun to tinker with.


  • Puzzle Games

    • Making players wrack their brain and make them want to tear out their hair trying to solve a tough puzzle is a pleasure only known to puzzle game devs.

    There must be some sort of satisfaction in being able to come up with a problem that can stump others.

    On the other side of the spectrum, these games offer an appealing reward system in the carthartic feeling of finally solving a brain teaser.

    This gives puzzle games a play cycle that keeps players hooked by constantly challenging them.


  • RPGs

    • RPGs are probably one of my most played game genres and as such I've grown to love the feeling of levelling up characters.

    They give players the oppurtunity to ignore some of their troubles for a while and live vicariously through a self insert.

    I have always wanted to develop my own RPG and create a grand adventure for players to enjoy.
